    About NB Flood Watch

    A real-time water level monitoring dashboard for New Brunswick rivers and streams. This is an unofficial community project — always refer to the official GeoNB River Watch for authoritative information.

    How to Use:

    • Click a station marker on the map or in the station list to view its water level chart.
    • The chart shows observed levels, forecast data, and WSC real-time readings over a ±3 day window centered on today.
    • Use the Layers button (left side) to toggle historical flood extents and switch basemaps.
    • Use the Stations button (bottom bar) to browse stations by location, name, or risk level.
    • Coloured threshold lines on each chart indicate advisory, watch, warning, flood, and record levels.

    Status Levels:

    • Normal — Below advisory threshold
    • Advisory — Rising, approaching watch level
    • Watch — Moderate flood risk
    • Warning — Significant flood risk
    • Flood — At or above flood stage
    • No Data — No recent readings available

    Data Sources & Acknowledgements:

    This project would not be possible without the following open data providers. Thank you!

    • GeoNB River Watch — Province of New Brunswick, Department of Environment & Local Government. Provides flood alert thresholds, forecast water levels, and station metadata via the River Watch Map XML feeds.
    • Water Survey of Canada (WSC) — Environment and Climate Change Canada. Provides real-time hydrometric gauge data via the EC GeoMet OGC API.
    • GeoNB Historical Floods — Province of New Brunswick. Provides historical flood extent and limit layers from 1973, 1976, 1987, 2008, and 2018 events.
    • Canada Province Boundary — Province boundary geometry via ArcGIS Online.
    • Leaflet — Open-source interactive mapping library.
    • Chart.js — Open-source charting library for the water level graphs.
    • Esri — World Imagery basemap tiles and ArcGIS services.
    • OpenStreetMap — Street Map basemap tiles.

    Disclaimer:

    This is an independent, unofficial tool. Data is fetched in real time from public sources and may be delayed or incomplete. Do not rely solely on this application for flood safety decisions. Always consult official Government of New Brunswick advisories and emergency management resources.
